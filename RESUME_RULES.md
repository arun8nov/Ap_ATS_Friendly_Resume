### Optimizing Content & Keywords
* **Mirror the Job Description:** Identify the top 5–10 skills/keywords in the posting and weave them naturally into your bullet points.
* **Standardize Section Titles:** Use "Work Experience" or "Professional Experience" rather than "My Journey" or "Career Highlights."
* **Use the Right Keywords:** If the job asks for "Project Management," don't just write "Managing Projects." Use the exact phrasing.
* **Include Acronyms & Longform:** Use both "Search Engine Optimization" and "SEO" to ensure you hit the keyword regardless of how the recruiter searches.

---

### Writing High-Impact Bullets
* **Start with Action Verbs:** Begin every bullet with a strong verb (e.g., *Spearheaded, Optimized, Negotiated, Developed*) rather than "Responsible for."
* **Quantify Everything:** Use numbers, percentages, and dollar amounts to prove your impact (e.g., "Increased sales by 22%" or "Managed a budget of $50k").
* **The "X-Y-Z" Formula:** Accomplished [X] as measured by [Y], by doing [Z]. 
* **Focus on Achievements, Not Tasks:** Don't list your job description; list what you actually won or improved while in that role.
* **Keep it Concise:** Bullets should be 1–2 lines maximum. If it's longer, it's a paragraph, and it won't be read.

---

### Final Polishing Rules
* **Check for Consistency:** Ensure all dates are formatted the same way (e.g., MM/YYYY or "March 2024").
* **No Personal Pronouns:** Avoid "I," "me," or "my." Start directly with the action verb.
* **Hyperlink your Portfolio:** Include a clean link to your LinkedIn or GitHub, but ensure the link is live and not just a text string.
* **Eliminate "Fluff":** Remove vague buzzwords like "Hard worker," "Team player," or "Detail-oriented." Let your quantified results prove those traits.
